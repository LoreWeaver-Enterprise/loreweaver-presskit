Story Angles

The following angles may be useful for journalists covering LoreWeaver. Each connects to broader industry trends.

The narrative tooling gap

Game studios spend an estimated €26,675 per gameplay hour on narrative content. Modern RPGs contain millions of words of script. Yet the industry's standard authoring tools have not changed fundamentally in over a decade. LoreWeaver addresses this gap with AI that puts writers in control rather than replacing them.

On device AI vs. cloud AI in games

Most AI tools for game development rely on cloud APIs. Director takes the opposite approach by running entirely on the player's hardware. This eliminates latency, removes ongoing costs for studios, and avoids sending player data to external servers. As small language models become more capable, on device AI opens new possibilities for real time narrative gameplay.

AI that supports creators instead of replacing them

Over 29,000 game industry jobs were cut between 2023 and 2025. Narrative designers faced a 19% layoff rate, the highest of any discipline. LoreWeaver positions itself as a tool that amplifies what writers can do rather than automating them away. Every AI extraction requires human review. The writer always has the final say.

AAA veterans building indie tools

The LoreWeaver team includes developers who shipped Hellblade, Dying Light 2, Horizon Forbidden West, Crusader Kings 3, and Stellaris. They experienced the narrative pipeline problem firsthand at major studios and built LoreWeaver to solve it for teams of every size.
