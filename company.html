LoreWeaver builds AI powered narrative tools for game developers. Game studios spend thousands of euros per gameplay hour on narrative content, and that cost multiplies when they localize to additional languages. At the same time, narrative designers face the highest layoff rates in the industry while players demand ever larger, more reactive story worlds.

LoreWeaver tackles this problem with two products. Architect converts raw lore, world bibles, and design documents into game ready narrative data: quests, dialogue trees, characters, and branching storylines. Director takes that structured data and generates live NPC behavior, dynamic plot, and adaptive dialogue at runtime, entirely on the player's device with no cloud dependency. Together they form the only end to end narrative pipeline from authoring to runtime. LoreWeaver operates as LoreWeaver B.V. from the Netherlands and was founded in 2025 by a team with credits on Hellblade, Dying Light 2, Horizon Forbidden West, Crusader Kings 3, and Stellaris.

The Mission

Game developers create some of the richest narrative experiences in any medium. Yet the tools available to them have not kept pace with the scale of modern game worlds. A single RPG can contain millions of words of dialogue, hundreds of quests, and thousands of interconnected characters.

LoreWeaver exists to give narrative teams the tools they need to build worlds at the scale players expect, without replacing the human creativity that makes those worlds worth exploring. Every feature puts the writer in control. The AI handles the repetitive, structural work so designers can focus on the stories that matter.

The Founding Story

LoreWeaver grew out of direct frustration with the state of narrative tooling in game development. The founding team brings experience from titles like Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice, Dying Light 2, Horizon Forbidden West, Crusader Kings 3, and Stellaris. They saw the same problem at every studio: brilliant narrative designers spending most of their time on structural busywork instead of creative storytelling.

When small language models became capable enough to run on consumer hardware, the team saw an opportunity to solve both sides of the problem at once. Architect handles the authoring side by turning raw lore into structured game data. Director handles the runtime side by generating live NPC behavior on the player's own device. No cloud dependency, no data privacy risk, no ongoing per token costs for the studio.

LoreWeaver B.V. was incorporated in the Netherlands in 2025. The team began building Architect first, and it entered beta in early 2026. Director pilot programs launched alongside it.

Industry Context

These figures provide context for the problem LoreWeaver solves. Journalists are welcome to cite them with attribution to LoreWeaver's market research.

  • Game studios spend an estimated €26,675 per gameplay hour on narrative content in the base language. That figure rises to €184,143 when localized across 12 languages.
  • Narrative designers experienced a 19% layoff rate in 2024, the highest of any game development discipline.
  • Over 29,000 game industry jobs were cut between 2023 and 2025.
  • AAA budgets now regularly exceed €200M per title.
  • Modern RPGs like Baldur's Gate 3 contain over 2 million words of script.
